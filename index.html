Quadratic Equations

Problem: Solve $x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0$ by Factorization

Step 1: Split the Middle Term

Find two numbers that multiply to $6$ and add up to $-5$. Those numbers are $-2$ and $-3$.

$$x^2 - 2x - 3x + 6 = 0$$

Step 2: Take Out Common Factors

Group the terms and factor them out:

$$x(x - 2) - 3(x - 2) = 0$$

$$(x - 2)(x - 3) = 0$$

Step 3: Find the Roots

Set each factor to $0$:

$$x - 2 = 0 \implies x = 2$$

$$x - 3 = 0 \implies x = 3$$

💡 Master Guide Note: Always verify your signs. A common trap is writing $(x+2)(x+3)$, which results in $+5x$ instead of $-5x$.
